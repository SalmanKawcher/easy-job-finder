Marketing, Sales & CX Jobs in the Gulf: A Guide for Candidates from South Asia

SK
Md Salman Kawcher, MSc, ACIM
Marketing & Customer Insights Specialist, 6+ years across Bangladesh, the UK and Portugal

Moving to the Gulf for a Marketing, Sales or Customer Experience role is one of the most common career steps for professionals from Bangladesh, India and the wider region. It is also a genuinely different job search from applying at home, and treating it the same way is where a lot of people lose time.

The boards are different

Bayt, GulfTalent and Naukrigulf carry a large share of Gulf roles that simply do not appear on the job boards you would use in Bangladesh or India. If you are searching from outside the region and only using your home-country boards plus LinkedIn, you are seeing a fraction of what is actually open. Go directly to the regional boards.

Sector concentration shapes everything

Gulf hiring is heavily concentrated in certain sectors: hospitality, retail, logistics, real estate and financial services. That concentration shapes which of your Marketing or Customer Experience experience reads as most relevant. When you search, pairing a role title with a sector filter, rather than searching the role title alone, narrows a huge noisy list down to roles you are genuinely likely to land.

Recruiters matter more here

A meaningful share of Gulf roles are filled through direct recruiter outreach and referrals before they are ever posted publicly. Recruitment agencies play a bigger role in this market than in many others. Making yourself visible to them, keeping your LinkedIn current and clearly stating the roles and locations you are targeting, is not optional if you want access to the full market.

Search smart on the regional boards

A few habits make Bayt, GulfTalent and Naukrigulf far more useful. Use exact-phrase search for role titles so you get relevant results rather than a broad mess. Combine role and sector in a single search. Filter for recently posted roles, because listings can sit live for weeks. And cross-check the same role on LinkedIn, since some employers post there first and the regional boards second, or the other way around.

Timing and speed

Gulf hiring often moves quickly once a role goes live, particularly when a recruiter is actively sourcing. Applying promptly, with a CV already tailored to the sector you are targeting, puts you in a much stronger position than a slow, generic application.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best way to find marketing or sales jobs in Dubai and the Gulf from abroad?

Go straight to the regional boards. Bayt, GulfTalent and Naukrigulf carry many Gulf roles that never appear on the boards you would use in Bangladesh or India.

Search by role and sector together. Gulf hiring is concentrated in sectors like hospitality, retail, logistics and finance, so pairing role and sector narrows a broad list to realistic matches.

Make yourself visible to recruiters. A significant share of roles are filled through direct outreach before ever being advertised, so agencies and a clear LinkedIn presence matter.

How do I search GulfTalent, Bayt and Naukrigulf effectively?

Use exact-phrase search for role titles so results stay relevant instead of returning a broad, noisy list.

Combine role and sector in a single search, since Gulf hiring is heavily sector-concentrated.

Filter for recently posted roles, because listings on these boards can remain live for weeks after being filled, and cross-check the same role on LinkedIn in case it was posted there first.
